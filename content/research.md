Modern medical imaging generates massive amounts of complex data. My research focuses on developing robust computational approaches to extract meaningful clinical insights, bridging the gap between raw data and actionable medical knowledge.

## Current Directions

### Deep Learning for Medical Image Segmentation
Accurate segmentation of medical images is a fundamental step in automated diagnosis and quantitative analysis. I apply state-of-the-art deep learning architectures to improve the precision, efficiency, and robustness of segmenting anatomical structures and anomalies across multiple imaging modalities.

### AI-Powered Workflows in BME
Beyond pure algorithm design, I am deeply interested in integrating cutting-edge AI technologies into biomedical research pipelines — from large language models to real-time brain-computer interfaces. By optimizing these computational frameworks, I aim to accelerate data processing and enhance the overall efficiency of biomedical engineering workflows.

## Projects

### Hippocampus Segmentation from Brain MRI via 3D U-Net
*Chinese Society of Biomedical Engineering Innovation Design Competition (Undergraduate Group) — National Third Prize*

Developed a 3D U-Net-based pipeline for hippocampal region segmentation from brain MRI scans. Unlike conventional 2D slice-by-slice approaches, 3D U-Net processes volumetric data directly, preserving spatial continuity and inter-slice contextual information for more accurate hippocampal segmentation. The hippocampus plays a critical role in neurological research, and accurate automated segmentation supports the study of neurodegenerative diseases such as Alzheimer's.

**Keywords:** 3D U-Net, Brain MRI, Hippocampus, Volumetric Segmentation

### Echocardiogram Segmentation via U-Net and ResT Transformer
*Undergraduate Thesis*

Combined U-Net with ResT, a Transformer variant, for echocardiogram image segmentation. The model leverages the local feature extraction capability of U-Net alongside the hierarchical attention mechanism of ResT to achieve accurate cardiac structure segmentation from ultrasound data.

**Keywords:** U-Net, ResT, Vision Transformer, Echocardiogram, Medical Image Segmentation

### EEG-Based Intent Recognition via FPGA
*National Graduate Electronics Design Competition, FPGA Track — National Third Prize*

Designed a real-time intent recognition system using EEG (electroencephalography) signals. The system classifies directional intent signals (up, down, left, right) from brainwave data and maps them to FPGA-controlled outputs, demonstrating a practical brain-computer interface pipeline.

**Keywords:** EEG, Brain-Computer Interface, FPGA, Signal Processing, Intent Classification
